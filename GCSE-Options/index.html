The Course At a Glance

MISSION: Equip students with logical and critical thinking skills necessary to succeed at IB and the world.

You will develop:

  • Computational thinking, that is thinking about what can be computed and how, and includes consideration of the data required
  • Understanding of the main principles of solving problems by using computers
  • Understanding that every computer system is made up of sub-systems, which in turn consist of further sub-systems
  • Understanding of the component parts of computer systems and how they interrelate, including software, data, hardware, communcations and people
  • Skills necessary to apply understanding to solve computer-based problems using a high-level programming language.

In Details

What the Course Offers


Section 1:
Computing Theory Study

    Theoretical knowledge from the following subject content:
    1. Data representation
    2. Communication and Internet technologies
    3. Computer systems
    4. Hardware and software
    5. Security
    6. Ethics

    Assessment:

    Short-answer and structured questions. Questions will be based on section 1 of the subject content. All questions are compulsory. No calculators are permitted. Externally assessed. 1 hour and 45 minutes, 75 marks, 60%.

Section 2:
Computational Thinking, Problem Solving & Programming

    Computational thinking, problem solving, paper based programming (Python), code tracing and applied computing as well as theoretical knowledge of Computer Science from the following subjects:
    1. Algorithm design and problem-solving
    2. Programming
    3. Database

    Assessment:

    Short-answer and structured questions. Questions will be based on section 2 of the subject content. All questions are compulsory. 20 marks are from questions set on the pre-release material. No calculators are permitted Externally assessed. 1 hour 45 minutes, 50 marks, 40%
